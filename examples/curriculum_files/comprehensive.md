# Science and Technology Grade 5 2024

## Essence Statement
Learners explore scientific concepts through hands-on experiments and develop technological literacy for the 21st century.

## General Learning Outcomes
- Understand basic scientific principles
- Apply scientific method to investigations
- Develop problem-solving skills
- Use technology responsibly

## STRAND 1: LIVING THINGS

### 1.1 Plants and Animals

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
- Identify characteristics of living things
- Classify plants and animals
- Describe life cycles of common organisms
- Explain adaptations to environment

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
- Observe plants and animals in school garden
- Create classification charts
- Draw and label life cycle diagrams
- Conduct habitat surveys

**Key Inquiry Questions:**
- What makes something alive?
- How do plants and animals differ?
- Why do organisms look different in different places?

**Core Competencies:**
- Critical thinking: Analyzing and classifying
- Communication: Presenting findings
- Creativity: Designing experiments
- Collaboration: Working in groups

**Values:**
- Respect: Caring for living things
- Responsibility: Environmental stewardship
- Unity: Working together

**PCIs:**
- Environmental education
- Health education
- Digital literacy

**Suggested Resources:**
- Magnifying glasses
- Plant and animal specimens
- Field guides
- Tablets for research

**Assessment Methods:**
- Observation checklists
- Practical demonstrations
- Written tests
- Project presentations

### 1.2 Human Body Systems

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
- Identify major body systems
- Describe functions of digestive system
- Explain importance of balanced diet
- Practice good hygiene habits

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
- Create body system models
- Track daily food intake
- Demonstrate proper handwashing
- Role-play healthy habits

**Key Inquiry Questions:**
- How does food give us energy?
- Why is hygiene important?
- What happens when we eat?

**Core Competencies:**
- Self-efficacy: Taking care of health
- Critical thinking: Understanding body functions
- Communication: Explaining health concepts

**Values:**
- Responsibility: Personal health
- Respect: Respecting our bodies
- Integrity: Making healthy choices

**PCIs:**
- Health education
- Nutrition awareness
- Life skills

**Suggested Resources:**
- Body system charts
- Food pyramid posters
- Hygiene supplies
- Health education videos

**Assessment Methods:**
- Oral questions
- Practical demonstrations
- Health journals
- Group discussions

## STRAND 2: ENERGY

### 2.1 Sources of Energy

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
- Identify different sources of energy
- Distinguish between renewable and non-renewable energy
- Explain importance of energy conservation
- Demonstrate safe use of energy

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
- Build simple solar ovens
- Create wind turbines from recycled materials
- Survey energy use at home
- Design energy-saving campaigns

**Key Inquiry Questions:**
- Where does energy come from?
- Why should we save energy?
- How can we use energy safely?

**Core Competencies:**
- Critical thinking: Analyzing energy use
- Creativity: Designing solutions
- Digital literacy: Researching energy topics

**Values:**
- Responsibility: Energy conservation
- Respect: Caring for environment
- Patriotism: Using local resources

**PCIs:**
- Environmental education
- Sustainable development
- Safety education

**Suggested Resources:**
- Solar panels
- Batteries
- Energy meters
- Recycled materials

**Assessment Methods:**
- Projects
- Presentations
- Written tests
- Practical demonstrations

|-----------|------------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| Scientific inquiry | Designs and conducts independent investigations | Follows scientific method with guidance | Participates in guided investigations | Struggles with basic procedures |
| Classification | Creates detailed classification systems | Classifies organisms accurately | Classifies with some errors | Cannot classify reliably |
| Communication | Presents findings clearly with evidence | Explains concepts adequately | Communicates with support | Has difficulty explaining |
| Practical skills | Demonstrates advanced techniques | Performs procedures correctly | Needs some assistance | Requires significant help |
